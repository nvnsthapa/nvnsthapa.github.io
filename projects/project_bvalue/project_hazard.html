Probabilistic Seismic Hazard Assessment of Central Nepal Himalayan Region

Probabilistic Seismic Hazard Assessment (PSHA) is a methodology for estimating the likelihood of earthquake occurrences in a specific region over a particular time period. In the context of Nepal's Himalayan region, PSHA is critical for understanding seismic activity and assessing associated risks. Nepal, located in the seismic hotspot of the Himalayas, faces significant earthquake hazards as a result of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates colliding. The devastating 2015 Gorkha earthquake highlighted the region's vulnerability, emphasizing the importance of conducting accurate seismic hazard assessments.

Seismic hazard map of Nepal (Pandey et al. 2002)

PSHA studies in Nepal's Himalayan region have provided valuable insights into the seismic hazard landscape, assisting with disaster preparedness, risk reduction strategies, and resilience building initiatives. However, advances in seismology, geology, and data analytics continue to improve these assessments, emphasizing the importance of regular updates to ensure the accuracy and relevance of seismic hazard information for the region's residents and stakeholders.
In this project the Probabilistic Seismic Hazard Assessment (PSHA) of the Central Nepal Himalayan Region provides a comprehensive understanding of the area's earthquake hazards. Using an updated earthquake catalog and advanced modeling techniques, the assessment provides valuable insights into the seismic risk landscape.

The assessment results are represented in a seismic hazard map, which specifically shows the Peak Ground Acceleration (PGA) hazard for a 10% chance of exceeding in 50 years. This map is a valuable resource for policymakers, engineers, urban planners, and other stakeholders involved in risk management and disaster preparedness.
