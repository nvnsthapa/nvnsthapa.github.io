Navin Thapa

Graduate Doctoral Researcher
CERI, University of Memphis
Namaste! I, Navin Thapa, am a Seismology-focused doctoral candidate in geophysics. Exploring the Earth's seismic activities and comprehending the dynamics of earthquakes and other geological phenomena are subjects that captivate my attention. Through the analysis of seismic data and participation in numerous seismic research projects, I have contributed to our knowledge of the Earth's structure and seismic hazards. My objective is to make a scholarly contribution to the field of seismology while simultaneously enhancing the well-being and capacity of communities residing in areas prone to earthquakes. We shall embark on an exhilarating expedition as we jointly delve into the mesmerizing realm of seismology. Over the years, a multitude of geoscientists have affirmed that the Earth possesses the quality of adaptability in the face of novel circumstances. One of the mechanisms underlying such alterations is earthquakes, which never cease to intrigue me. With a strong commitment to fulfilling my life's purpose of aiding both the local community and the global community, I made the deliberate choice to undertake a doctoral program in seismology. Nepal, from which I originate, is situated in the central region of the Himalayas, an area characterized by an active tectonic orogeny that renders it highly susceptible to perpetual seismic vulnerability, shallow focus, and large earthquakes. This singular occurrence has sparked my interest in the discipline of seismology. My professional trajectory commenced at the Central Department of Geology at Tribhuvan University and has since transitioned to the Center for Earthquake Research and Information (CERI) at the University of Memphis. I am honored to be a member of the Earthquake Physics Group (EPG) at CERI.
